    Bilbao

    & Biscay coast

    The Guggenheim, the old Seven Streets, and a green hinterland of cliffs, villages and the Urdaibai reserve.

    Guggenheim Museum

    Must-see
    Bilbao centreIn town• good with kids

    Frank Gehry’s titanium-clad icon that turned Bilbao from industrial port to art capital. 35,000 titanium sheets shift colour with the light; inside, world-class contemporary art.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Outside it’s free to see Jeff Koons’ flower-covered “Puppy” and Louise Bourgeois’ giant spider “Maman”.
    Guide tip. Walk the riverside from the Arenal bridge (1.5 km, ~20 min) or take the tram from the Arriaga stop.

    Casco Viejo — the Seven Streets

    Must-see
    Casco ViejoIn town• good with kids

    The historic heart of Bilbao, the “Zazpi Kaleak”. Narrow lanes packed with shops, tavernas and the city’s oldest sights — the cathedral, Arriaga theatre and the Ribera market.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Start here on foot; everything in the old town is a short stroll apart.

    Plaza Nueva pintxo crawl

    Must-see
    Plaza NuevaIn town

    Bilbao’s grandest square, ringed by 64 arches. Under the arcades sit some of the city’s best pintxo bars — perfect for a no-walking bar-hop.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Local favourites: La Olla, Charly, Sorginzulo, Víctor Montes.
    Guide tip. Order one pintxo + a small drink per bar, then move on. That’s the local rhythm.

    San Juan de Gaztelugatxe

    Must-see
    UrdaibaiOn the way

    One of Northern Spain’s most iconic places: a tiny hermitage on a rocky islet, reached by a winding stone causeway and 241 steps. (Dragonstone in Game of Thrones.)

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Go early and wear good shoes; the climb is steep but the views are unforgettable.

    Urdaibai Biosphere Reserve

    Must-see
    UrdaibaiOn the way• good with kids

    A UNESCO biosphere of estuary, marsh and cliffs. Make it a full day along the ~55 km coastal route from Gaztelugatxe east to the village of Ea.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. The wetlands are one of the Basque Country’s richest birdwatching areas.

    Gernika

    Must-see
    GernikaOn the way

    A town of huge symbolic weight: bombed in 1937 (the inspiration for Picasso’s “Guernica”). Visit the Peace Museum, the Tree of Gernika and the historic Casa de Juntas.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Refuel afterwards with a Basque lunch at Boliña el Viejo or pintxos at Taberna Auzokoa.

    Lekeitio

    Must-see
    LekeitioOn the way• good with kids

    A handsome coastal town halfway between Bilbao and San Sebastián: a colourful harbour, a mystic island, fine beaches and an old quarter of noble houses.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. At low tide a seaweed causeway lets you walk to San Nicolás island — but watch the tide so you’re not stranded!
    Guide tip. Isuntza beach is calm for kids; Karraspio has the waves.

    Oñati — “the Basque Toledo”

    Must-see
    OñatiOn the way• good with kids

    A monumental inland town named a top world destination in 2023. Wander Plaza de los Fueros, the San Miguel church and the 1548 Sancti Spiritus University.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Sweet tooth? Txokolateixia is a chocolate museum with tastings.

    Artxanda funicular

    Top pick
    ArtxandaIn town• good with kids

    A 1915 funicular that climbs 300 m up Mount Artxanda for a sweeping viewpoint over the whole city and surrounding hills.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. €4.80 return. Departs every 15 min from Plaza del Funicular.
    Guide tip. A 15-min walk up top brings you to Txakoli Simón, a grill restaurant for meat lovers.

    Mercado de la Ribera

    Top pick
    Casco ViejoIn town• good with kids

    The largest covered market in Europe (10,000+ m²) on the river. Locals shop, Michelin chefs source, and the upstairs gastrobars let you taste Basque cooking.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Go upstairs for pintxos with a market view.

    Museo de Bellas Artes

    Top pick
    Bilbao centreIn town

    One of Spain’s most complete art museums — 10,000+ works from Goya, El Greco, Ribera and Picasso to the present. Quieter and more classical than the Guggenheim.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Right beside Doña Casilda park, so pair the two.

    Azurmendi

    Top pick
    LarrabetzuIn town

    Eneko Atxa’s three-Michelin-star temple: a 25-course tasting menu blending innovation with deep respect for Basque culture and sustainability.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. ~€300 per person, 20 min from Bilbao. Book well ahead.

    Bilbao’s classic pintxo bars

    Top pick
    Casco Viejo & EnsancheIn town

    Beyond Plaza Nueva: Bar Bacaicoa on Plaza Unamuno (the famous “chorizo to hell”), cheese-lover’s Gaztandegi and oyster bar El Puertito on García Rivero, plus Café Iruña and the timeless La Viña del Ensanche.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. García Rivero street is a pintxo run in itself.

    Urdaibai Bird Center

    Top pick
    UrdaibaiOn the way• good with kids

    Interactive museum and research centre on the 250+ bird species that live in or migrate through the marshes. Spot rarities from the hides with an English audioguide.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. €8 adults, €5 children.

    Laida & Laga beaches

    Top pick
    UrdaibaiOn the way• good with kids

    Two of Biscay’s finest beaches in the heart of the reserve. Laida’s calm estuary side is ideal for kids; wild, open Laga is framed by high cliffs and loved by surfers.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Hugely popular in summer — arrive early for a parking spot.

    Bosque de Oma (painted forest)

    Top pick
    UrdaibaiOn the way• good with kids

    Artist Agustín Ibarrola painted hundreds of pine trunks into a living canvas that shifts as you move. A ~2.8 km walk in; the full loop is about 7 km.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Free, but reserve a free ticket online. Park at Santimamiñe by restaurant Lezika.
    Guide tip. A fun change of pace for families during an Urdaibai day.

    Vizcaya Hanging Bridge

    Top pick
    Getxo–PortugaleteOn the way• good with kids

    The world’s oldest transporter bridge (1893), a UNESCO site linking Portugalete and Getxo. Cross by gondola, or take the lift to the walkway 61 m above the sea.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. 20 min from central Bilbao.

    Gorliz beach

    Top pick
    GorlizOn the way• good with kids

    One of the safest, prettiest Basque beaches — a sheltered shell-shaped bay with calm water, showers, playgrounds and picnic spots. A great family day.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. A 3.2 km path leads through pine woods to the province’s highest lighthouse.

    Salto del Nervión

    Top pick
    Monte SantiagoOn the way

    The highest waterfall on the Iberian Peninsula (220 m), plunging into a deep canyon. A nearly flat forest walk leads to the clifftop platform.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Best in spring after rain/snowmelt; often dry in summer. Walk is 6–9.4 km return depending on car park.
    Guide tip. Even when dry, the beech-wood walk and canyon views are worth it.

    Santuario de Arantzazu

    Top pick
    ArantzazuOn the way

    A striking example of Basque avant-garde religious architecture, set among rock massifs and ravines. A scenic 9 km route from Oñati reaches it.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Nearby Arrikrutz is a 15 km cave system (guided visits in Spanish).

    Catedral de Santiago

    Casco ViejoIn town

    Bilbao’s oldest church, 600+ years old, on Plaza de Santiago. Note the Pilgrim’s Gate used by walkers on the Camino de Santiago, and the 15+ chapels inside.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. €8 entry includes audioguide and access to the nearby San Antón church.

    Teatro Arriaga & Arenal bridge

    Bilbao centreIn town

    A photogenic neo-baroque theatre (1890) named after the “Spanish Mozart”. Cross the 1845 Arenal bridge linking the old town to the modern Gran Vía.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Check the programme — you might catch opera or classical music in the chandelier-lit hall.

    Basílica de Begoña

    Bilbao centreIn town

    The locals’ favourite church, atop Artagan hill. Climb the 323 steps of the Calzadas de Mallona from lively Plaza Unamuno (~20 min).

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Prefer not to climb? Take the paid lift in the Casco Viejo metro station, then 7 min on foot.

    Azkuna Zentroa (La Alhóndiga)

    Bilbao centreIn town• good with kids

    A former wine warehouse reimagined by Philippe Starck in 2010: 43 artistic columns, a cinema, library and a rooftop pool with a glass floor.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Free to wander in and admire the columns.

    Itsasmuseum & Carola crane

    Bilbao centreIn town• good with kids

    A maritime museum on the old Euskalduna shipyards, with the great red Carola crane outside. Ships, navigation and Athletic Bilbao football history.

    Open in Maps

    Parque Doña Casilda

    Bilbao centreIn town• good with kids

    Bilbao’s green lung, the 1907 “Duck Park”, with 1,500+ plant species and a big pond. An easy break with kids.

    Open in Maps

    Sopela & Barrika beaches

    Sopela & BarrikaOn the way

    The surf mecca of the Basque coast: dramatic cliffs and four beaches. At Barrika, low tide reveals the famous “Dragon’s Tail” flysch rock formation.

    Castillo de Butrón

    Elantxobe

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Eat at Kraken for a burger with a sea view.

    Ea

    EaOn the way• good with kids

    San Pedro de Atxarre viewpoint

    UrdaibaiOn the way

    Urdaibai’s finest lookout: a 1.5 km climb (~30 min) to a 15th-c. hermitage at 300 m, with a vast panorama over the estuary and marshes to Mundaka.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Pick a clear day.

    Urkiola & Mirador de las Tres Cruces

    UrkiolaOn the way• good with kids

    A monastery in a beech-and-birch natural park. A 15-min beech-lined path reaches the “Three Crosses” viewpoint over the Atxarte gorge.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. The easy Saibi loop (5.2 km, ~1 hr) suits the whole family.

    Gomiztegi cheese farm

    GomiztegiOn the way• good with kids

    Just before Arantzazu, a prize-winning family cheese farm. See the sheep and taste the cheeses — fun for kids and food lovers alike.

    Open in Maps

    Costa Verde

    Asturias’ green coast

    A road trip of hidden coves, sea-geysers, dinosaur footprints and pretty fishing towns like Llanes and Tazones.

    Mirador del Fito

    Must-see
    Sierra del SueveOn the way• good with kids

    A 360° panorama at 597 m taking in sea, valleys, the high mountains and villages — from Lastres and Ribadesella to Covadonga and the Picos de Europa beyond.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. 200 m below the top (sea side) is a quiet picnic area with a great sea view.

    Playa de Gulpiyuri

    Must-see
    Naves (Llanes)On the way• good with kids

    The “world’s smallest beach” — a 40 m strip of sand about 100 m inland, fed by the sea through a hole in the rock. A natural monument and a magical natural pool.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. A few minutes’ walk from the car park (€3/day).
    Guide tip. Nearby Sidrería Llagar Cabañón serves home-made cider and grilled meat in an old farmhouse.

    Bufones de Pría

    Must-see
    Llames de PríaOn the way

    Spectacular “sea geysers”: waves force seawater up through holes in the limestone cliffs, blasting spray up to 20 m high with a loud snorting sound.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Best in autumn/winter when seas are rough and at high tide. 15–20 min walk from La Rotella or Garaña car parks.
    Guide tip. Keep well back — water can surge up without warning.

    Tazones

    Must-see
    TazonesOn the way• good with kids

    One of Asturias’ prettiest fishing villages: cobbled lanes, heritage fishermen’s houses, and the shell-covered “Casa de las Conchas”. Superb fish and seafood.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. At low tide you can spot dinosaur footprints on the village beach.
    Guide tip. Easy to explore in 1–1.5 hr, including lunch.

    Llanes old town & harbour

    Must-see
    LlanesIn town• good with kids

    A handsome fishing town: a medieval centre (El Torreón tower, the gothic basilica), grand “Indianos” mansions, and the colourful “Cubos de la Memoria” art-block breakwater.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Walk the clifftop Paseo de San Pedro to the La Talá lookout for the best view over the town.

    Playa de Torimbia

    Top pick
    Niembru (Llanes)On the way

    A half-moon bay rated by Lonely Planet among Spain’s best beaches — 500 m of golden sand wrapped in green hills, with clear shallow water.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. No facilities outside summer. Park near Cabo Prieto and walk down.
    Guide tip. Don’t miss the sunset.

    La Cuevona of Cuevas

    Top pick
    Cuevas (Ribadesella)On the way• good with kids

    A 300 m cave that is literally the gateway to the village of Cuevas — you pass through it to reach the houses. Lit walkway past dramatic stalactites and stalagmites.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. There’s also a 10 km “route of the mills” loop from the car park.

    Playa de Rodiles

    Top pick
    VillaviciosaOn the way• good with kids

    One of Asturias’ best beaches: a 1 km golden strand backed by pine and eucalyptus woods with boardwalks and picnic tables — and a renowned surf spot.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. The forest behind the beach is a lovely shaded picnic spot.

    Playa de la Griega & Jurassic Museum

    Top pick
    ColungaOn the way• good with kids

    Guide tip. Free to wander among dinosaur replicas in the museum gardens — great for kids.

    Llanes coastal cliff walk

    LlanesOn the way

    Playa de Ballota & Mirador de la Boriza

    Picos de Europa

    Asturian high country

    Covadonga’s lakes and sanctuary, the remote village of Bulnes, and the kingdom of Cabrales cheese.

    Cangas de Onís

    Must-see
    Cangas de OnísIn town• good with kids

    The gateway to the Picos: the first capital of the Kingdom of Asturias, famous for its much-photographed “Roman bridge” hung with the Victory Cross.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Sunday market under the arcades by Palaciu Pintu — Asturian cheeses, sausages, honey.
    Guide tip. Eat cachopo at La Sifonería; the compact old town takes 1.5–2 hr on foot.

    Lagos de Covadonga

    Must-see
    CovadongaIn town• good with kids

    The most famous place in the Picos: the glacial lakes Enol and Ercina, ringed by peaks. A scenic 6.4 km loop walks between them, accompanied by mountain cattle.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. In high season (June–mid Oct, holidays, most spring/autumn weekends) reach them only by shuttle: €9 return, timed slot, €2 parking.
    Guide tip. Bring layers, water and snacks — at 1,100 m the weather turns fast.

    Santuario de Covadonga

    Must-see
    CovadongaIn town• good with kids

    One of Spain’s great pilgrimage sites: the Holy Cave with the image of “La Santina”, and the rose-marble Basilica of Santa María la Real. Marks the start of the Reconquista.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Museum €3. Climb the 103-step “Stairway of the Promise” to the cave.

    Bulnes

    Must-see
    Poncebos / BulnesOn the way• good with kids

    A remote, beautiful village at 649 m, ringed by 1,600 m limestone walls — and not reachable by car. Pure mountain quiet, fresh air and big views.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Funicular from Poncebos: 7 min, every 30 min (10:00–20:00 high season). Or hike the steep old goat path.

    Ruta del Cares

    Must-see
    Poncebos / CaínOn the way

    Spain’s most famous gorge walk, carved into the cliffs between Poncebos and Caín — a spectacular path high above the Cares river.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Start early; it’s exposed and busy. Las Arenas de Cabrales is the natural base.

    Buferrera mines & Mirador de Entrelagos

    Top pick
    CovadongaOn the way• good with kids

    Part of the lakes loop: from the Pedro Pidal visitor centre, walk past old mine workings to the Mirador de Entrelagos, the iconic viewpoint over both lakes at once.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Pick up a route map at the centre — phone signal is poor on the loop.

    Asiego & Mirador Pedro Udaondo

    Top pick
    Asiego (Cabrales)On the way

    Las Arenas de Cabrales

    Las Arenas de CabralesIn town• good with kids

    Costa Cantábrica

    Cantabria

    Medieval Santillana, Gaudí in Comillas, the Altamira caves and the wild Costa Quebrada.

    Santillana del Mar

    Must-see
    Santillana del MarIn town• good with kids

    The “town of three lies” (not holy, not flat, not by the sea) — a perfectly preserved medieval village of cobbled lanes, noble houses and flower-filled balconies, declared a National Monument.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Don’t miss the Romanesque Colegiata de Santa Juliana and its cloister of 42 carved capitals.
    Guide tip. Park at Plaza del Rey or behind the tourist office; the centre is car-free.

    Cuevas de Altamira

    Must-see
    Santillana del MarIn town• good with kids

    The “Sistine Chapel of Palaeolithic art”: cave paintings of bison and horses up to 36,500 years old. The original limits visitors, but the “Neocueva” replica is near-indistinguishable.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. €3; free under 18 and over 65; free Sat from 14:00 and all day Sunday. Reserve in high season.

    San Vicente de la Barquera

    Must-see
    San Vicente de la BarqueraIn town• good with kids

    A medieval seaside town with bobbing fishing boats, long beaches and the Picos de Europa as a backdrop. Two great pilgrim routes (Camino del Norte and Lebaniego) cross here.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Castillo del Rey castle + museum €2, with panoramic views.
    Guide tip. Crossing the long 15th-c. Puente de la Maza, the “Bridge of Wishes”, frames the classic town view.

    Comillas — Gaudí’s El Capricho

    Must-see
    ComillasIn town

    An open-air museum of Catalan modernism by the sea. The star is Gaudí’s playful El Capricho (1883–85), with ceramic sunflowers and a green-and-red minaret.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. El Capricho €7 (audioguide). Next door: Palacio de Sobrellano €5 and the Pontifical University €5.

    Cueva El Soplao

    Must-see
    RionansaOn the way• good with kids

    A natural wonder called the “underground Sistine Chapel” — rare cave formations and cave-pearls amid 20+ km of galleries, with an industrial mining heritage.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Standard tour €14.50 (€11.50 over-65s); adventure tour €35. Book ahead; ~12–13 °C inside, can be slippery.

    Carmona

    Top pick
    CarmonaOn the way

    A hidden jewel in the Cabuérniga valley — one of Spain’s prettiest, least-known villages, with 16th–18th c. mountain architecture and traditional clog-makers (“albarqueros”).

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. The Mirador Asomada del Ribero gives the best panorama over the village.

    Bárcena Mayor

    Top pick
    Saja-BesayaOn the way• good with kids

    One of Cantabria’s oldest and loveliest villages — the only inhabited settlement in the Saja-Besaya park, with rustic 16th–17th c. houses and famous mountain stew (cocido montañés).

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Gateway to Saja-Besaya, the largest beech forest in Europe. Parking €2 in high season.
    Guide tip. An easy 7.5 km forest loop (Fuente Clara) starts from the village.

    Costa Quebrada — Valdearenas & the dunes

    Top pick
    LiencresOn the way• good with kids

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Calm Playa de Usil nearby is ideal for a day out with kids.

    Playa de la Arnía

    Top pick
    LiencresOn the way

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Enjoy it from above at Restaurante El Cazurro for good value and a great view.

    Valles Pasiegos road trip

    Top pick
    Valles PasiegosOn the way• good with kids

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Runs Liérganes → Puente Viesgo (or reverse).
    Guide tip. In Selaya, taste the local sweets — sobaos and quesada pasiega.

    Puente Viesgo — Monte Castillo caves

    Top pick
    Puente ViesgoOn the way• good with kids

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Start of the Vía Verde del Pas, a flat former-railway cycle path.

    Natural Park Oyambre & beaches

    OyambreOn the way• good with kids

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. A clifftop section of the Camino del Norte links San Vicente and Comillas (12.2 km).

    Mirador de La Picota

    MorteraOn the way

    Liérganes

    LiérganesOn the way

    One of Spain’s prettiest villages and the gateway to the Pasiego valleys: cobbled streets, noble houses with flower-filled balconies and a photogenic stone bridge.

    Open in Maps

    La Concha beach & promenade

    Must-see
    San SebastiánIn town• good with kids

    Often called Europe’s finest city beach — 1,300 m of golden sand in a shell-shaped bay, framed by the iconic white balustrade and Belle Époque mansions.

    Open in Maps
    Guide tip. Stroll the full promenade from the old town to Ondarreta.

    Parte Vieja pintxo route

    Must-see
    Parte ViejaIn town

    The pintxo capital of the world. Hop bar to bar in the old town with a txikito (small wine) or zurito (short beer). Each bar a specialty.

    Open in Maps
    Good to know. Legends: Borda Berri (stewed cheeks, Idiazábal risotto), Ganbara (crab tartlet), Bar Néstor (tortilla — only two a day, 13:00 & 20:00), Txepetxa (anchovies), La Mejillonera (mussels), La Viña (cheesecake).

    Hondarribia
